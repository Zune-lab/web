Epipelagic

The Sunlight Zone (0m - 200m)

🌡️ 12°C - 20°C ☀️ 100% Light ⚖️ 1 atm

The vibrant top layer where photosynthesis occurs. Teeming with 90% of all marine life.

Mesopelagic

The Twilight Zone (200m - 1000m)

🌡️ 4°C - 12°C 🌘 Faint Light ⚖️ 20 - 100 atm

Sunlight rapidly fades into darkness. Bioluminescence begins to appear to confuse predators.

Bathypelagic

The Midnight Zone (1000m - 4000m)

🌡️ 4°C 🌑 Pitch Black ⚖️ 100 - 400 atm

No sunlight reaches this depth. The only visible light comes from the creatures themselves.

Abyssopelagic

The Abyss (4000m - 6000m)

🌡️ 2°C - 3°C 🌑 Pitch Black ⚖️ 400 - 600 atm

A freezing, crushing environment. Life here is sparse, surviving entirely on deep-sea snow falling from above.

Hadalpelagic

The Trenches (6000m - 11000m)

🌡️ 1°C - 2°C 🌑 Pitch Black ⚖️ 600 - 1100 atm

The deadliest environment on Earth. Pressure here is equivalent to an elephant standing on your thumb.
